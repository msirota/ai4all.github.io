2019-2024 UCSF AI4ALL Program

Founded in 2019 by Dr. Marina Sirota, the UCSF AI4ALL program recruits high school students for a tuition-free, three-week summer experience. The inaugural program launched as an in-person experience. In 2020, co-led by Dr. Sirota and Dr. Tomiko Oskotsky, the program shifted to a synchronized virtual format and remained online through 2024. Students learned core AI concepts through faculty lectures, gained hands-on research experience through projects, and engaged with a breadth of role models working at the intersection of computer science and biomedicine. Mentoring, along with career and personal development sessions with faculty, researchers, industry professionals, and students, supported growth and career exploration. More details about these program activities are available in an article about the program (Oskotsky et al., PLOS Computational Biology, 2022) and the program blog on Medium.com

Each year, the program culminated in a Symposium with student presentations of their group research projects. Recordings of the Keynote Speech and Student Presentations are available for 2019 , 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024.
